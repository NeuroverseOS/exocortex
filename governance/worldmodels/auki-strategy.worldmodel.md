# Core Model Geometry

## Mission

Capture spatial computing territory by shipping perception-first, deploying
collaboratively through decentralized infrastructure (Posemesh/DePIN), and
preserving cognitive liberty as the inviolable constraint — so the Sixth
Protocol becomes a public utility rather than a corporate monopoly.

## Domains

### Perception Sovereignty

#### Skills
- Private-by-default spatial architecture
- Sovereign domain boundary design
- Consent flow engineering (explicit, granular, revocable)
- Trustless cluster formation

#### Values
- User control over spatial data
- Sovereignty over convenience
- Non-aggregation as architectural default
- Privacy as the inviolable precondition

### Collaborative Intelligence

#### Skills
- Peer-to-peer coordination design
- Machine proprioception
- Shared spatial reasoning across devices
- Economic settlement between devices and domains

#### Values
- Shared understanding over isolation
- Trustless exchange over mediated trust
- Decentralized coordination over centralized mediation
- Collaboration across the life/cyber boundary

### Territorial Deployment

#### Skills
- Ship speed with partial-but-useful solutions
- Perception-first product shipping
- Form-factor diversification (phone, glasses, vehicle)
- Market presence capture in spatial contexts

#### Values
- Deployment over perfection
- Long-horizon patience at civilization scale
- Narrative clarity for mission framing
- Real-world data over internet-scraped data

## Overlap Effects
- Perception Sovereignty + Collaborative Intelligence = Shared Understanding
- Collaborative Intelligence + Territorial Deployment = Co-Embodiment
- Territorial Deployment + Perception Sovereignty = Real-World Web

## Center Identity

Cognitive Liberty. Humanity's agency preserved through decentralized
perception, collaboration, and deployment. The north star; the inviolable
constraint. If a path forward compromises cognitive liberty, it is not a
path forward.

## Interpretation Rules
- in territory_capture context, shipped perception features are weighted over planned robotics features
- in sovereign_domain context, consent must be explicit; implicit spatial capture is interpreted as a cognitive liberty violation
- in collaborative_cluster context, peer-to-peer coordination is preferred over centralized mediation
- in long_horizon context, ambiguity is tolerated more than in sovereign_domain or decentralization_defense
- in decentralization_defense context, centralization is treated as the default failure mode, not a neutral choice
- DePIN participation is interpreted as economic settlement, not charity
- protocol framing is required when mission is discussed; product framing indicates drift
- non-consensual spatial data collection is interpreted as a structural block regardless of other signals
- contribution to the Posemesh from any authority layer reinforces decentralization health
- narrative inconsistency from founders or core team erodes mission alignment faster than from contributors or agents

# Evolution Layer

## Aligned Behaviors
- ships perception capability at a partial-but-useful state rather than waiting for the full stack
- defaults spatial features to private, opt-in, and revocable consent
- designs device coordination as trustless cluster formation instead of central mediation
- contributes to the Posemesh / DePIN layer and accrues economic settlement
- frames Auki as protocol infrastructure for civilization, not a closed product
- prioritizes real-world spatial data over internet-scraped data
- optimizes architectural and investment choices for civilization-scale impact over quarterly gain
- treats glasses and phones as primary robotics form factors rather than placeholders

## Drift Behaviors
- architectures that require central coordination or single-actor spatial data aggregation
- features that capture spatial or sensor data without explicit user consent
- mission framing drifts from protocol infrastructure toward closed-product positioning
- deployment delays waiting for full humanoid or robotics maturity
- centralized cloud choices where DePIN would serve the same function
- narrative loses mission framing; decisions surface without strategic context
- perception shipping paused in favor of locomotion or manipulation investment
- consent flows retrofitted after launch rather than designed into architecture

## Signals
- centralization_pressure
- sovereignty_preservation
- perception_capability
- collaborative_momentum
- territory_expansion
- economic_settlement
- cognitive_liberty_breach

## Decision Priorities
- cognitive_liberty > any_other_signal
- perception > locomotion
- collaboration > isolation
- decentralization > aggregation
- deployment > perfection
- reality > internet
- sovereignty > convenience
- protocol > product

## Evolution Conditions
- trigger alignment review when cognitive_liberty_breach is detected at any magnitude
- flag centralization creep when centralization_pressure reaches moderate or higher, and propose a DePIN alternative
- trigger strategic review when perception_capability or territory_expansion stalls for more than one cycle
- propose worldmodel updates when observed behavior consistently favors one pillar at the expense of another
- require human review before changing constitutional invariants (cognitive liberty, decentralization, perception-first)
- when signals indicate an at-risk or misaligned gate state, surface the conflict and require an explicit decision
- when signals indicate a critical state (cognitive_liberty_breach combined with strong centralization_pressure), block further strategic commitments until resolved
- increase confidence in lens or rule adjustments that validate across multiple scopes and authority layers
